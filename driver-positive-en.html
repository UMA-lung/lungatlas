Stage IV NSCLC First-Line Therapy
Driver Mutation–Positive

Treatment decisions should be grounded in primary literature. This page presents key Phase III trial data supporting each regimen, with guideline recommendations listed below. Comprehensive genomic profiling (CGP) is the starting point — the specific mutation detected directly determines prognosis and treatment choice.

PFS data published in NEJM 2024 (HR 0.70); final OS analysis published NEJM September 2025. This is the first Phase III trial to demonstrate both PFS and OS benefit of amivantamab (EGFR-MET bispecific antibody) + lazertinib (3rd-gen EGFR-TKI) over osimertinib monotherapy. Median OS is projected to exceed osimertinib by more than one year.

🇯🇵Japan (GL 2025): Added as recommended 1st-line for exon19del/L858R. Approved March 2025.
🇺🇸NCCN v4.2026 / ASCO Living GL: Category 1. FDA approval 2024. ASCO Living GL updated through 2026.
🇪🇺ESMO 2025: Grade A recommendation. EMA approval early 2025.

Randomized 2:2:1 to amivantamab+lazertinib, osimertinib, or lazertinib alone. Subgroup analyses suggest attenuated OS benefit in patients ≥65 years (MD Anderson, E. Singhi). Requires IV infusion, complicating outpatient management. VTE risk is elevated; anticoagulation prophylaxis is recommended.

Adding platinum-based chemotherapy to osimertinib extended PFS by ~9 months. The final OS analysis (NEJM 2026) confirmed an OS benefit: 47.5 mo vs 37.6 mo (HR 0.77, p=0.02). Toxicity burden (hematologic, nausea) increases substantially; selection for PS 0–1 patients with high CNS metastasis risk is practical.

🇪🇺ESMO: Recommended for PS 0–1 non-squamous.

The first 3rd-generation EGFR-TKI to demonstrate OS benefit in the 1st-line setting. Significant PFS benefit in CNS metastasis subgroups. Remains the most widely used reference standard. ADAURA trial also established osimertinib as adjuvant therapy post-resection (DFS HR 0.17).

🇯🇵Japan: Continued Category 1 recommendation. Adjuvant use (ADAURA) also covered by insurance.
🇺🇸NCCN / ASCO: Category 1 for 1st-line and adjuvant settings (upgraded 2025).
🇪🇺ESMO: Standard of care. Choice between osimertinib and MARIPOSA should be individualized based on patient profile and healthcare setting.

The first Phase III trial to demonstrate benefit for EGFR exon20 insertion, a subset for which conventional EGFR-TKIs are largely ineffective. Accurate identification via CGP is essential for treatment selection.

🇺🇸NCCN: Category 1. Exon20 insertion explicitly categorized separately from exon19del/L858R.

Presented at ASCO 2024 (Solomon et al., JCO 2024). With median PFS not reached at 5 years, this represents the longest PFS ever reported for any single-agent targeted therapy across all metastatic solid tumors. 50% of lorlatinib patients remained on treatment at 5 years. Asian subgroup analysis (JTO 2025) confirmed similar results (5-yr PFS 63%).

🇯🇵Japan (GL 2025): Highest-level recommendation for ALK-positive 1st-line treatment.
🇺🇸NCCN: Category 1. 5-year data further solidifies lorlatinib as preferred 1st-line agent.
🇪🇺ESMO: Grade A recommendation. Particularly valuable in patients with or at high risk for CNS metastases.

J-ALEX (Lancet 2017) established alectinib as an effective and well-tolerated option in Japanese patients with ALK+ NSCLC, and it remains widely used in Japan. The cognitive and metabolic side effects of lorlatinib require careful monitoring. An ongoing debate concerns sequencing strategies: starting with alectinib and switching to lorlatinib upon progression vs. using lorlatinib upfront.

Taletrectinib substantially reduces CNS toxicities (chronic dizziness, balance impairment, fall risk) that plagued earlier-generation ROS1 inhibitors such as crizotinib and entrectinib, while maintaining high response rates and intracranial activity. QOL improvement is the primary differentiator of this next-generation agent.

🇺🇸NCCN: Added following FDA approval summer 2025. Reduced dizziness noted as key QOL advantage.
🇪🇺ESMO 2025: Recommended as new agent for ROS1-positive NSCLC.

KRAS G12C was long considered "undruggable" until the development of covalent inhibitors (sotorasib, adagrasib). No significant OS difference (HR 1.01) generated debate, but PFS benefit and tolerability favor targeted therapy over docetaxel. First-line positioning under investigation.

More prevalent in older patients with smoking history, particularly lung adenocarcinoma and sarcomatoid carcinoma. Detection requires CGP. Should be actively suspected in elderly smokers testing negative for EGFR/ALK.

CGP testing covers: EGFR · ALK · ROS1 · HER2 · KRAS G12C · MET exon14 · RET (selpercatinib) · NTRK (entrectinib) · BRAF V600E (dabrafenib+trametinib). Each alteration has a matched targeted therapy.

Diagnosis to First-Line Treatment: Standard Flow

1
Pathologic diagnosis + Adequate tissue acquisition
Confirm squamous vs non-squamous histology. Obtaining sufficient tissue for CGP and PD-L1 testing is the critical first step.
2
Comprehensive Genomic Profiling (CGP) + PD-L1
Simultaneously test for EGFR · ALK · ROS1 · HER2 · KRAS G12C · MET exon14 · RET · NTRK · BRAF V600E. Measure PD-L1 TPS concurrently.
